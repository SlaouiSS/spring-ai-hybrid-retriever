BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) is a pretrained
language model based on the transformer encoder architecture. It uses multi-head
self-attention to build contextual representations of tokens, processing the
entire input sequence in parallel rather than left-to-right.

Pretraining on masked language modelling and next-sentence prediction gives BERT
general linguistic knowledge. Fine-tuning on downstream tasks then adapts those
representations to specific applications without retraining from scratch.

The transformer encoder stacks multiple attention layers. Each layer computes
scaled dot-product attention: query, key, and value matrices derived from the
input are used to weigh the contribution of every token to every other token's
representation. Multi-head attention runs several such projections in parallel
and concatenates their outputs.

BERT tokenises input using WordPiece, splitting rare words into subword units
to control vocabulary size. The [CLS] token's final representation is often
used as a fixed-size sentence embedding, though mean pooling over all tokens
generally produces better sentence-level similarity scores.
