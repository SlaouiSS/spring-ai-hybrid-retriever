BM25 is a probabilistic ranking function used in information retrieval. It improves
on the classic TF-IDF formula by adding document length normalisation and a term
frequency saturation factor that prevents high-frequency terms from dominating
the score disproportionately.

The BM25 score for a query term t in document d is computed as:
  score = IDF(t) * tf * (k1 + 1) / (tf + k1 * (1 - b + b * |d| / avgdl))
where k1 controls term frequency saturation and b controls length normalisation.

Lucene has used BM25 as its default similarity since version 8, replacing the
earlier TF-IDF implementation. The StandardAnalyzer tokenises input, lowercases
terms, and removes stop words before building the inverted index. At query time,
the inverted index maps each term to the list of documents containing it, allowing
fast BM25-ranked retrieval without scanning every document.

Elasticsearch and OpenSearch also default to BM25 over an inverted index. Keyword
search with BM25 excels at exact term matching, rare technical terms, and product
codes — cases where semantic similarity models often fail because the terms are
absent from training data.
